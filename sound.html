The soundscape, performed and recorded by a violinist, translates each gesture into an acoustic state. Every movement activates a distinct emotional register, while the on-screen visualization amplifies and extends the sensation through motion and rhythm.


Open Hand → Joy
A bright and resonant melody unfolds. The violin opens into warmer tones and fluid phrasing, suggesting expansion and lightness. The visual composition responds with balanced, harmonious movement, reinforcing the feeling of presence and vitality.

Semi Closed Hand → Sadness
The melody becomes slower and more fragile. Notes stretch and hesitate, evoking melancholy and introspection. On screen, the visual structure fragments and drifts, mirroring the disorientation and subtle instability that often accompany sadness.

Closed Fist → Fear
A sharper, higher-pitched sound emerges, tense and insistent. The violin compresses into anxious frequencies that suggest urgency and vulnerability. The visualization accelerates and becomes more frenetic, supporting the sensation of pressure and unease.

This project explores the hand as a primary form of expression. Before language, the body already communicates through gesture. Here, digital environments make minimal movements visible, translating them into emotional states.

The work unfolds across three dimensions: text, sound and space. Each responds to the same three gestures, open hand, half-closed hand and closed fist, associated with joy, sadness and fear.

In the textual dimension, gesture alters the legibility and structure of a poem. In the sound dimension, a violin composition shifts in tone and intensity, supported by a reactive visualization. In the spatial dimension, the interface expands, destabilizes or compresses, reflecting how emotion shapes the way space is perceived and inhabited.

The proposed classification of gestures and emotions remains intentionally open. It serves as a demonstrative framework rather than a universal system, suggesting that meaning can emerge from gesture while remaining multiple and interpretative.
